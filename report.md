# Executive Revenue & Retention Report

## 1. Revenue Trends
* **Summary:** The business has shown significant growth over the past year, with monthly revenue increasing from approximately **$229,540** in April 2025 to a peak of **$1.87M** in December 2025.
* **Key Insight:** The highest Month-over-Month (MoM) revenue growth occurred in March 2026, reaching **61.26%**, following a recovery from a seasonal dip in January.
* **Observation:** The 30-day moving average reveals a steady daily revenue baseline that grew from ~$8,000 in early April to much higher volumes by the end of the year.

## 2. Customer Retention (Cohort Analysis)
* **Strongest Cohort:** The **March 2026** cohort shows the highest immediate engagement with a **99.49%** 30-day retention rate.
* **Consistent Performers:** Cohorts from late 2025 (October to December) maintained strong 30-day retention rates above **54%**, peaking at **67.33%** in December.
* **Retention Drop-off:** We observed a significant drop-off in the **May 2025** cohort, where retention fell to **39.71%** by the 90-day mark, suggesting a need for improved long-term engagement for early-summer recruits.

## 3. Category Performance
* **Top Performer:** **Electronics** is our dominant category, starting with **$112,646** in April 2025 and reaching a running total of **$479,561** by June 2025 alone.
* **Supporting Categories:** **Sports** and **Home & Kitchen** are strong secondary drivers, both maintaining consistent growth and contributing significantly to the running revenue totals.
* **Growth Trend:** **Electronics** revenue grew from $112K in April to over $156K in June, representing the core of our market share.

## 4. Strategic Recommendations
1. **Capitalize on Electronics:** Since Electronics is the primary revenue driver, we should allocate more marketing budget to high-margin tech products in the next quarter.
2. **Loyalty Program:** Focus on the "February and March" high-retention cohorts. Implement a loyalty program to maintain their exceptionally high initial engagement (90%+) into the 60 and 90-day windows.
3. **Address Seasonal Dips:** The sharp revenue drop in January 2026 (-58.13%) suggests a post-holiday slump. We recommend planning a "New Year" clearance or promotion to stabilize volume during this period.
